UniStream: A 48kHz Universal Streaming Audio Codec with Flow-Regularized Multi-Expert Quantization

Anonymous Authors · Interspeech 2026 Submission

Abstract

We present UniStream, a fully causal 48 kHz universal audio codec supporting speech, music, and environmental sound within a single model. UniStream introduces Multi-Expert Residual Vector Quantization (ME-RVQ), which routes each frame to two of four specialized codebooks, improving PESQ by 0.21 over a single-codebook baseline with only 7.3% parameter overhead. To further regularize the quantized latent space, we employ Optimal Transport Conditional Flow Matching (OT-CFM) exclusively during training as a zero-inference-cost regularizer. At 12 kbps, UniStream reduces speech Mel-Distortion by 37% relative to EnCodec; at 22.5 kbps it achieves the lowest causal Mel-D on speech and environmental audio among all evaluated systems.
